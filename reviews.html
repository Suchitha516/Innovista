Alumni - School of Computer Science
★★★★☆

I graduated from CMR University's Computer Science program in 2024, and looking back, I'm grateful for the education I received. The curriculum was industry-relevant with good emphasis on practical skills and coding. Most professors were knowledgeable and approachable. The programming labs are well-equipped, though they could use some updates on certain software. What really helped me was the internship program in the final year - I was placed with a good tech company which eventually offered me a full-time position. The campus has a vibrant tech community with hackathons and coding competitions. Some courses could use more depth, but overall, a solid foundation for a tech career.

Arun Krishnan
Student - School of Architecture
★★★☆☆

I'm currently in my third year of B.Arch program. The architecture studio facilities are decent but could be improved with more space and better equipment. Some professors are excellent with practical industry experience, while others rely too much on theoretical teaching. The design software we use needs updating to the latest versions. On the positive side, the field trips and site visits are well organized and provide valuable hands-on experience. The design workshops with visiting faculty from renowned architectural firms are a highlight. The library needs more architecture-specific books and journals. Campus Wi-Fi can be unreliable at times, which is frustrating when working on large digital projects.

I visited CMR University City Campus as part of my professional assessment of educational institutions in Bangalore. The campus infrastructure is impressive - modern classrooms, well-maintained grounds, and good laboratory facilities. The administrative staff was cooperative and transparent during my visit. The curriculum across various programs appears to be well-structured with a good balance of theory and practical components. Based on my assessment, CMR University stands as a strong option for higher education in Bangalore.
